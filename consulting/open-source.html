    A: All Rights Reserved; though free public repositories are for open source projects.

    When you create a new repository, you have the option to select an Open Source license (and a .gitignore) to initially commit to the new repository.

  • Q: Do you have to include a license notice with every source file?

    A: No. A LICENSE (.txt, .md (Markdown), .rst (ReStructuredText), or similar is sufficient to indicate that the copyright is anything other than All Rights Reserved, by default.
